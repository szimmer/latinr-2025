Our training approach

Self-paced learning

  • Curated list of free learning resources

  • Paid subscriptions to learning platforms

  • Self-paced learning works well for basics
  • Learning platforms are mostly virtual and big hurdle was “OK, how do I do this on MY computer with MY data?”

Webinars showing off open-source

  • These were not tutorials

  • Provided demos, resources, and examples

  • Very good for demonstrating workflows common to our group

  • We don’t do much modeling
  • Mostly data cleaning, sampling, weighting

Create cohort of mentors

  • Staff with open-source experience

  • Mentors were not all advanced users

  • Mentors monitored an email, Slack channel, and Yammer pages for questions

  • Mentors hosted office hours

  • Mentor group is VERY enthusiastic, we’ve got a Teams channel going and discuss successes and questions for each other regularly

  • Office hours were our biggest success with mentors which I will detail

Office hours (1.0)

  • Started to fill in the gap of self-paced learning

  • Intention: staff bring questions

  • Reality: mentors have topics in their back pocket

  • Not recorded

  • Lots of discussion of how SAS & R think differently, vector operations vs program data vector
  • Initially thought recording would scare people into NOT asking questions
  • Had lots of lurkers/observers/wallflowers
  • People couldn’t consistently attend and kept wanting to go back to basics

Office hours (2.0)

  • Mentors prepare material, lean towards tutorials

  • Recorded

  • Materials shared on enterprise GitHub (sneaky way to try to push this too…)

  • Created a mini project of work similar to our team’s typical tasks

  • reduced frequency from twice a week to once a week
  • email sent out ahead with topic planned for the day
  • allowed asycnhronous participation (complicated scheduling)
  • feedback that people are having trouble mapping generic training to their every day work
  • this is where we are now for office hours, lots of engagement

Conversion approach

Pilot projects

  • Small set of projects/tasks converted

  • More detail in: useR 2025 - Navigating the Transition: Lessons from Adopting an Open-Source Approach - Carlos Petzold

Demonstrate success

  • Show off projects already using open-source or recently converted

  • Have staff discuss their experiences

  • Demonstrate tasks (not even full projects) that are using R
  • Use staff that already know R (mostly earlier career staff)

Building our own tools

  • In-house package developed for codebook creation

  • Package developed for survey sampling, SampleSelectR

  • Cheatsheets for comparing R and SAS for common tasks

  • AI tool made specifically for SAS to R conversion
